Abstract

This project proposes a dynamic initiative to promote ecotourism across the United States, using Colorado as its initial pilot stage. Through the creation of a series of short films that combine powerful narratives with artificial intelligence technology, the project will highlight natural beauty, cultural diversity (including the heritage of indigenous communities), and the importance of environmental conservation. It promotes sustainable tourism and generates a positive social impact by integrating the "Your Green Footprint" program, which turns tourists into active agents of environmental restoration through the symbolic planting of native species. This initiative enriches the cultural and tourism sector, drives the socioeconomic development of the involved regions, and creates job opportunities for local communities

This proposal is supported by the author's comprehensive professional portfolio, which demonstrates the necessary expertise for successful execution. The portfolio includes academic credentials in Social Communication and Audiovisual Journalism, technical specializations in cinematography (including directing, screenwriting, and editing), and documented international recognition through awards and official selections at film festivals for the short film "El Bombillo" This evidence underscores the author's capacity in audiovisual production, narrative development, and project leadership, ensuring both the artistic quality and operational viability of the ECO Reels initiative.

Keywords: artificial intelligence; CO₂ capture; Colorado river; ecological restoration; ecotourism; environmental conservation; filmmaking; indigenous communities; national parks; professional development portfolio; short film and documentary films; social communication; sustainable tourism; United States.

Project Description

This project, titled "ECO Reels: Sowing Green Awareness Through Ecotourism in the U.S.," is a dynamic and flexible initiative, subject to adjustments in its structure, order, and development methodology. These changes respond to the need to adapt to opportunities and challenges that may arise during its implementation, thus ensuring its effectiveness and reach.

The project aims to promote ecotourism in the United States, initially focusing on the state of Colorado, through the creation of a series of short films that combine powerful narratives with artificial intelligence (AI) technology. These short films will highlight natural beauty (geographical, ancestral, flora and fauna), including cultural diversity and the importance of environmental conservation, fostering sustainable tourism and generating positive social impact.

Social Objectives and Economic Benefits

  • Improved Quality of Life: By promoting ecotourism, the project contributes to the preservation of natural areas and the economic development of local communities.
  • Job Creation: During its development, the project will create employment opportunities in areas such as audiovisual production, technology, tourism, and conservation.
  • Innovation and Technology: The integration of AI tools allows for efficient and high-quality production, even with limited resources.
  • Ecological Restoration and Collective Awareness: Involves tourists in practical conservation actions through authorized planting of native species.
